Here's a scenario: You've paid €120 for a Champions League ticket, €150 for flights, and €90 a night for a hotel. Three days before the match, your club draws the tie or the match is postponed due to a waterlogged pitch. You try to claim on your travel insurance and discover that "event cancellation" isn't covered under your standard policy.

This happens to football fans regularly. And the reason is simple: most travel insurance is designed for people going on holiday, not people whose entire trip is built around one specific event on one specific day.

This is what you actually need to know.

---

## Why standard travel insurance usually isn't enough

Standard travel insurance covers the obvious stuff: medical emergencies, lost luggage, trip cancellation if *you* get ill. What it typically doesn't cover:

- The match being postponed or cancelled by the organising body
- Your team being knocked out of the competition before the match
- The event being moved to a different date or venue
- Ticket costs if you can't attend for reasons covered by your policy

That last point is particularly frustrating. Some policies will pay out if you personally can't travel due to illness, but they won't reimburse the cost of the ticket itself because a ticket to a sporting event is classified differently to transport or accommodation.

---

## What to actually look for

When you're buying travel insurance for a trip built around a football match, these are the specific things to check:

**1. Event cancellation or curtailment cover**
Does the policy explicitly cover losses if the event you're attending is cancelled or postponed? Some specialist sports travel policies include this. Standard policies from comparison websites usually don't.

**2. Ticket cost included in cancellation payout**
If the trip is cancelled for a covered reason (your illness, a family emergency), does the policy reimburse pre-purchased event tickets or only transport and accommodation?

**3. Sports-related medical cover**
This matters particularly for World Cup 2026 in the USA. Medical costs in America are extraordinary — a single night in hospital can cost more than the entire rest of your trip. Make sure any policy you buy has at minimum €500,000 medical cover for USA travel. Some specialists go to €1,000,000.

**4. Trip delay cover**
If your flight is delayed and you miss kick-off, does the policy compensate for the match you paid to attend? Some do, most don't.

## When is it worth buying?

For a €200 domestic away trip — probably not necessary unless you have a specific reason to worry about medical cover.

For a €600+ Champions League away trip — yes, absolutely. The ticket alone is probably €80-200. If the match is postponed and you have to rebook flights and hotels, a policy that pays out saves you hundreds.

For a World Cup 2026 trip to the USA — non-negotiable. Medical costs in America are in a different category. Buy a policy with substantial medical cover before you travel.

---

## What doesn't matter when buying travel insurance

A few things that get too much attention:

**The brand of the insurer** — Big name insurers aren't automatically better. Some of the best sports travel policies come from specialist providers most people haven't heard of. Read the policy, not the logo.

**The price** — A €9 policy from a comparison site and a €15 specialist sports policy are not equivalent products. The difference in coverage can be enormous. Don't buy on price alone.

**The "24/7 helpline"** — Every policy advertises this. It's table stakes. What matters is whether they'll actually pay out.

---

## The one thing to do before you buy

Read the exclusions. Not the marketing copy — the actual exclusions section of the policy document. Specifically look for:

- Pre-existing conditions
- Alcohol-related incidents (this matters for football travel)
- Civil unrest or disorder (relevant if you're going to a high-risk fixture)
- Ticket/event costs

If a specific scenario you're worried about is excluded, that policy is not right for your trip, regardless of how cheap or reputable it is.

---

Travel insurance for football trips is not complicated. But it does require buying the right product for what you're actually doing. Spend 20 minutes reading the policy document and you'll know immediately whether it covers what you need it to cover.
