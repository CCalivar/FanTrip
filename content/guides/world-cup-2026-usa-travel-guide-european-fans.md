Most European fans attending the 2026 World Cup will be making their longest football trip to date. The USA is a different proposition to a weekend in London or Munich — longer flights, a bigger country, different costs, different logistics. 

This guide is specifically for European fans making the trip. Here's what you need to know.

---

## The flight situation

From most European cities, you're looking at 8-10 hours to the East Coast (New York, Miami) and 10-12 hours to the West Coast (Los Angeles, San Francisco, Seattle). This is not a weekend trip — you need at least a week to make the travel time worthwhile.

**Key routes and airlines:**

- Madrid / London / Amsterdam to New York: Direct flights on multiple airlines. Iberia, British Airways, United, Delta. From €500-900 return in economy.
- Madrid / London to Miami: Direct flights. Similar prices.
- Madrid / London to LA: Direct or one stop. Slightly more expensive, longer journey.
- Madrid to Mexico City: Direct on Iberia and Aeromexico. One of the cheapest transatlantic options for European fans.

**When to book:**

Book as early as possible. Transatlantic routes to the same cities where World Cup matches are played will see significant price increases as the tournament approaches. Flights booked 8-12 months out are typically 30-50% cheaper than those booked 3 months out.

If you're flexible on dates, the cheapest flights are usually Tuesday-Thursday departures and arrivals. Weekend flights to New York or LA are always more expensive regardless of the World Cup.

## Which city to base yourself in?

The 16 host cities are spread across a huge geography. Trying to attend matches in multiple cities means internal US flights, which add cost and complexity.

**For European fans, the most practical bases:**

**New York / New Jersey** — MetLife Stadium hosts the Final and 7 other matches. Best-connected city from Europe with the most direct flights. Expensive — New York hotel prices are already high and will increase during the tournament. Great city for a World Cup trip.

**Miami** — Hard Rock Stadium, 6 matches. Great weather in June, more affordable than New York, good flight connections from Europe especially from Spain. The vibe for a football trip would be excellent.

**Mexico City** — Estadio Azteca (the legendary ground), 5 matches including the Opening. The most affordable option for European fans — flights from Spain are among the cheapest transatlantic routes, hotels are significantly cheaper than US cities, food and daily costs are lower. The atmosphere for football in Mexico is extraordinary.

**Los Angeles** — SoFi Stadium, 7 matches including a semifinal. Long flight, high costs, but iconic city. Worth it if the matches are right.

**My recommendation for most European fans:** pick one city, see 2-3 group stage matches there, and treat it as a proper trip to that city rather than trying to follow your country through the tournament.

## The cost of everything in the USA

The US is expensive by European standards, and it's been getting more so. Here's a realistic budget for a 7-day World Cup trip based in New York or LA:

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Return flights from Europe | €600-950 |
| Hotel (7 nights, city-centre) | €1,200-2,000 |
| 3 Group stage match tickets | €350-600 |
| Food and drink (7 days) | €500-800 |
| Local transport | €150-250 |
| Travel insurance | €40-60 |
| eSIM (North America) | €15-25 |
| **Total** | **€2,855-4,685** |

Mexico City is significantly cheaper across the board — flights from Madrid are often under €400 return, hotels are €60-120 per night, and daily costs are dramatically lower. The same trip from Madrid to Mexico City might cost €1,800-2,500 total.

## The money situation

**Use a zero-fee card.** This cannot be emphasised enough for the USA. Standard bank card foreign transaction fees of 3-5% on a €3,000 trip is €90-150 quietly going to your bank. A Revolut or Wise card costs nothing and uses the real exchange rate.

**Tipping culture.** The USA has a tip culture that Europeans find disorienting. At restaurants: 18-20% is standard, anything less is noticed. At bars: €1-2 per drink. At hotel restaurants: same as regular restaurants. Stadium food concessions and fast food: tipping is less expected but tip jars are everywhere. Budget for this — on a €500 food and drink week, realistically add €80-100 for tips.

**Cash vs card.** Most places in the USA accept card for everything. But keep $50-100 in cash for situations where card isn't accepted — some food trucks, some smaller venues.

## Connectivity — this is critical

Your European number will have high roaming charges in the USA, and you need reliable data for maps, tickets, messaging and everything else.

Buy a North America eSIM before you travel. Airalo offers North America multi-country plans that cover the USA, Canada and Mexico — essential if you're attending matches in Mexico City and then flying to a US city. A 5GB plan costs around €8-12.

Activate it when you land. You'll have local US data rates from the moment you're through customs.

**Also important:** Screenshot your tickets and save them offline before you travel. Stadium WiFi in the USA is variable, and you don't want to be loading a PDF in a car park on US cellular data.

## What to expect in US stadiums

American football stadiums are the primary venue for most World Cup matches, and they're configured differently to European football grounds:

**The good:** excellent facilities, wide concourses, good food options, well-organized. Most US stadiums have invested heavily in fan experience.

**The different:** the atmosphere at American sports events is more performance-driven — DJs, video screens, announcements — than the organic fan noise of a European ground. For a World Cup match, the mix of international fans will create something different to a regular NFL game.

**Security:** expect airport-style security. No large bags, prohibited items list applies strictly, arrive early to get through.

**Capacity:** these are large stadiums. MetLife seats 82,500. Navigation inside can be complex. Know your gate before you arrive.

---

## The things worth spending money on

**A hotel with air conditioning.** June and July in Miami, Dallas, or LA is hot. This is not optional.

**Travel insurance with strong medical cover.** US medical costs are genuinely shocking. A single night in an American hospital can cost €10,000+. Buy a policy with at minimum €500,000 medical cover. Don't cut corners on this.

**Pre-booked transport from the airport.** Match day rideshare in US cities near stadiums will be surging. Either book a transfer in advance with a fixed price or plan to use the metro/rail if available.

---

The World Cup comes to North America once every few decades. If you have the means and the opportunity to go, go. But go prepared — the logistics are more complex than a European away trip, and the fans who enjoy it most will be the ones who sorted everything before they landed.
