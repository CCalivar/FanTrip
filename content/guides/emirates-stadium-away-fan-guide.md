The Emirates is one of the best-run grounds in European football. Clean, modern, efficient, with good sight lines from almost every seat. It's also in North London, which means transport is straightforward, the surrounding area has plenty of options for pre-match, and the crowd — while not the loudest in England — generates decent atmosphere for big European nights.

Here's what away fans need to know.

---

## Getting tickets

Arsenal away tickets for Premier League matches are hard to get through official channels — you need a registered account and often accumulated loyalty points. For most visiting fans, the secondary market is the realistic route.

For Champions League matches at the Emirates, away fans get an allocation in the Clock End. These are distributed through your club's official supporter channels first, then the secondary market. Buy early — Champions League nights at the Emirates sell out completely.

Secondary market prices: Premier League matches against top clubs run €80-150. Champions League knockout rounds start at €100 and escalate based on the opposition and the stage.

---

## Getting to the Emirates

The Emirates is in Holloway, North London. The most convenient tube station is **Arsenal** on the Piccadilly line — it's a five-minute walk from the ground and the station was literally named after the club. On match days the station is Arsenal-only exit and well managed.

**From central London:**
- King's Cross: Piccadilly line, 4 stops, about 8 minutes
- Oxford Circus: Victoria line to Highbury & Islington, then overground or walk (20 min)
- Liverpool Street: approx 30-40 minutes by tube

**From Heathrow:** Piccadilly line direct to Arsenal station. About 55 minutes, no changes.

Avoid Uber on match days near the stadium — traffic is gridlocked for 45 minutes after the final whistle. The tube is always faster.

---

## Before the match: where to go

**The Tollington, 1 Hornsey Road** — the main away fan pub close to the ground. It fills up quickly for big matches so arrive early. Decent beer, no frills, does the job.

**The Highbury Barn** — slightly further from the ground but worth the walk for better beer and a less crowded experience. On Highbury Barn road, about 15 minutes walk.

**Drayton Park area** — several pubs within walking distance of the stadium. The area is mixed home and away fans for most matches.

Note: for certain high-risk fixtures (North London derby, Manchester United, etc.) some pubs near the ground are home fans only. Check before you go.

---

## Inside the Emirates

**Away end location:** The away section is in the upper tier of the Clock End (east end of the stadium). You're behind the goal, high up, but the sight lines are fine.

**Concourse:** Modern, well-stocked, accepts card. The food is average stadium fare — standard hot dogs and pies. Nothing special. Eat before you arrive.

**Atmosphere:** The Emirates has a reputation for being quieter than Highbury used to be. Big European nights change this — the atmosphere for a Champions League knockout match is considerably better than a routine Premier League game. Away fans in the Clock End generally have a good atmosphere regardless.

**Security:** Bag checks and wand searches on entry. No re-entry once you're in. Arrive 45 minutes before kick-off minimum for big matches — the queues at away turnstiles can be slow.

---

## After the match

Away fans are held briefly after the final whistle — usually 10-15 minutes — to allow the home crowd to disperse. This is standard practice at English grounds for certain fixtures.

Once released, most fans head back to Arsenal tube or walk to Highbury & Islington for the Victoria line. The area is well-policed after big matches. The walk back through Islington to Angel or Old Street is pleasant in summer and gets you away from the main crowd quickly.

---

## Where to stay

**King's Cross / Islington area** — best combination of tube access to the Emirates, good restaurants and hotels at various price points. 15-20 minutes to the ground.

**Central London** — most visitors stay central and use the tube. Anywhere on the Piccadilly or Victoria line is convenient.

For Champions League matches: book your hotel as soon as the fixture is confirmed. London hotel prices spike significantly for major European nights.

---

## The honest summary

The Emirates is a good ground for away fans — better than its reputation suggests. The match experience is professional, transport is easy, and for a Champions League knockout match, the atmosphere is genuinely good. The pre-match options in Islington are excellent.

Sort your ticket early, get there on the tube, and enjoy North London for the evening.
