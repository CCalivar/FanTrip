Planning a football trip is not complicated, but the order matters. Book the wrong things in the wrong order and you either end up with a ticket but no hotel, flights you can't use, or a weekend that costs twice what it should have.

Here's the process that works.

---

## Step 1: Confirm the fixture (before you do anything else)

This sounds obvious, but it catches people out every year. Before booking anything, make sure:

- The match is confirmed and has a date and time
- The fixture hasn't been moved for TV scheduling (this happens constantly in the Premier League and Champions League)
- You know which round it is — knockout match dates can shift if a replay is needed

For Champions League knockout matches specifically: the two legs are confirmed after the draw. Book based on those confirmed dates, not on speculation about which match you'd rather attend.

---

## Step 2: Decide on the ticket first, then build around it

The ticket is the hardest thing to get and the most time-sensitive. Everything else can wait 24-48 hours. The ticket cannot.

**Check in this order:**
1. Official club allocation (your club or the home club, depending on which end you want)
2. Official UEFA portal for Champions League/Europa League
3. Secondary market (StubHub, Viagogo, Ticketmaster)

Set a budget for the ticket before you start looking. The secondary market will show you a range and it's easy to rationalise spending more than you planned when the match is a few weeks away and supply looks limited.

Once you have a confirmed ticket — download it, screenshot it, note the entry gate and the seat reference.

---

## Step 3: Flights — book immediately after the ticket

Flight prices to cities with major football matches spike after high-profile fixtures are confirmed. A Champions League semifinal draw announcement will cause immediate price increases on routes to those cities.

**What to book:**
- Direct flights if available — one less thing to go wrong
- Outbound: day before the match (not morning of — travel disruption is real)
- Return: morning after the match or the following day — don't rush the return

**What to watch:**
- Refundable/changeable fares for knockout rounds — if your team is knocked out before the match and you don't want to go, you want an option. Usually costs 20-30% more than a fixed fare.
- Baggage — most people doing a 2-3 night trip can manage with a cabin bag. Saves time and cost.

---

## Step 4: Hotel — book on a cancellable rate immediately

Same logic as flights. Hotel prices in host cities for major matches spike fast.

**What to look for:**
- Free cancellation rate (slightly more expensive but worth it for knockout rounds)
- Location: close to public transport that goes to the stadium, not necessarily right next to the ground
- For a 2-night trip: check-in the evening before the match, check-out the morning after

**What to avoid:**
- Non-refundable rates for knockout matches (if the match is postponed, you're stuck)
- Hotels right outside the stadium — they're premium priced for the location and often worse value

---

## Step 5: Sort the practical stuff (one week before)

This is the admin that takes 20 minutes and saves hours of stress on the day:

**Money:**
- Load your Revolut or Wise card — don't wait until you're at the airport
- Have €30-50 in local currency for cash situations

**Connectivity:**
- Buy and install an eSIM for the destination country
- Airalo or YeSIM — takes 5 minutes, costs €5-8

**Documents:**
- Passport / ID valid (obvious, but check)
- Ticket downloaded and screenshot saved offline
- Hotel confirmation saved offline
- Emergency contact numbers noted

**Route:**
- Look up how to get from your hotel to the stadium on the local metro/transport app
- Know which entrance you're using (check your ticket for gate details)
- Know roughly how long it takes

---

## Step 6: Match day — execute the plan, don't improvise

Match day is not the time to figure things out. Everything should already be decided:

- When you're leaving the hotel (allow more time than you think you need)
- How you're getting to the stadium (public transport, not taxi/rideshare — too slow)
- What time you want to be through the turnstiles (45 minutes before kick-off minimum)
- Where you're going after the match

The fans who have the worst experiences at away games are usually the ones trying to sort things out on the day. The fans who have the best time are the ones who've done the thinking in advance and can just enjoy being there.

---

## The master checklist

Print this or save it. Use it for every away trip.

**When you decide to go:**
- [ ] Fixture confirmed with date and time
- [ ] Ticket sourced — downloaded, screenshotted, gate noted

**Immediately after:**
- [ ] Flights booked
- [ ] Hotel booked (cancellable rate)

**One week before:**
- [ ] Travel insurance purchased (if needed)
- [ ] Revolut/Wise loaded
- [ ] eSIM bought and installed
- [ ] All documents valid
- [ ] Route to stadium researched

**Day before travel:**
- [ ] Ticket screenshot saved offline
- [ ] Hotel confirmation saved offline
- [ ] Stadium gate and entry instructions noted
- [ ] Local emergency number noted

**Match day:**
- [ ] Leave hotel with margin
- [ ] Public transport to stadium
- [ ] Through turnstiles 45 min before kick-off

That's it. Everything on that list takes less than 2 hours total across the weeks before the match. In exchange, you spend match day relaxed, prepared, and watching football.

That's what these trips are supposed to be.
