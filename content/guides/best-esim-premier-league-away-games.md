## What's an eSIM and why does it matter for away trips?

An eSIM is a digital SIM card built into your phone. Instead of swapping a physical SIM, you download a data plan from a provider, activate it, and your phone switches to local rates in that country.

For a weekend away game in England, this means:
- Local UK data rates instead of EU roaming charges
- Keep your normal number active for calls (eSIM runs alongside your regular SIM)
- No hunting for a SIM card shop when you land
- Activate it before you leave — it's ready when you land

For a typical weekend in London or Manchester, you need maybe 2-3GB of data for maps, messaging, ticket display and the odd search. That's €4-8 on a decent eSIM. The same usage on roaming could cost €20-40 depending on your plan.

## Does my phone support eSIM?

Most modern smartphones do. If you bought your phone in the last 3-4 years, it almost certainly supports eSIM. The main exceptions are some budget Android devices and iPhones bought in mainland China.

Quick check: on iPhone, go to Settings → General → About and look for "Digital SIM." On Android, it's usually under Settings → Network & Internet → SIM cards.

If your phone doesn't support eSIM, a cheap local SIM from a UK supermarket (Tesco, ASDA) or convenience store costs £5-10 and is easy to find.

---

## When to activate it

Activate before you land. Most eSIMs can be installed in advance and set to activate when your phone detects the local network. This means when you land at Heathrow or Manchester Airport, your data is already working.

Don't activate in the airport — you don't need the data until you're through arrivals and heading to the hotel anyway. But do install it on the plane.

## What about for a multi-city European trip?

If you're doing multiple matches in different countries in the same trip — say London and then Dortmund the following week — buy separate eSIMs for each country, or look for a "Europe" plan that covers multiple countries.

Airalo has Europe multi-country plans from around €8-10 for 3GB that cover 30+ European countries. For World Cup 2026 with matches in the USA, Canada and Mexico, there are North America plans that cover all three countries in a single eSIM.
