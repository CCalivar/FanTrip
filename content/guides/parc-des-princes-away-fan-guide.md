Paris is one of the great cities in the world and the Parc des Princes is one of the most recognisable grounds in European football. For away fans, it's a genuinely good experience — the stadium is atmospheric, the city is excellent, and getting around is straightforward once you understand the metro.

Here's the practical guide.

---

## Tickets

PSG play in one of the best stadiums in Europe for atmosphere. The ultras sections (Virage Auteuil behind one goal, Virage Boulogne behind the other) create intense noise for big matches. Away fans are housed in a section of the Virage Boulogne end.

**Official channels:** Away allocations for Champions League matches go through the visiting club's supporter system. Apply through your club's official channels as soon as the fixture is confirmed.

**Secondary market:** StubHub and Viagogo list PSG Champions League tickets. For big knockout matches, expect €90-180 for the away section. Neutral tickets for high-profile matches (semifinals, for example) run €200-400+.

One important thing: French ticket systems can be strict about ID matching. Some PSG Champions League tickets are personal and non-transferable. Check the ticket terms before you buy on the secondary market.

---

## Getting to the Parc des Princes

The stadium is in the 16th arrondissement of Paris, in the southwest of the city. The metro is the correct way to get there.

**Metro:** Line 9 to **Exelmans** or **Marcel Sembat** (about 5-10 min walk), or Line 10 to **Porte de Saint-Cloud** (5 min walk). The Porte de Saint-Cloud exit puts you directly at the south end of the stadium.

**From central Paris:**
- From Gare du Nord: approx 35-40 min by metro (multiple route options)
- From Châtelet-Les Halles: Line 10 direct, about 25 minutes

**From CDG Airport:** RER B to central Paris, then metro. Total journey around 60-70 minutes.

Do not attempt to drive or take a taxi to the stadium on match days. Traffic around the 16th arrondissement is terrible and the walk from where you'd end up is longer than from the metro.

## Before the match in Paris

The stadium is in a residential area that doesn't have a natural pub scene nearby. Most away fans spend the pre-match either in central Paris or in the area around **Porte de Saint-Cloud**, which has several bars.

**Central Paris options:**
- Marais district (3rd/4th): excellent bars and restaurants, easy metro access to stadium
- Saint-Germain-des-Prés (6th): classic Parisian experience, more expensive
- Pigalle area (9th/18th): livelier, more casual bars

The practical reality: have your meal and drinks in central Paris, then take the metro to the ground with about 90 minutes to spare.

**Eating before a match in Paris:** Paris has hundreds of excellent casual options. A brasserie meal before a match is one of the genuine pleasures of a Paris away trip. Budget €15-25 for a solid sit-down meal at lunch, less for a quicker option.

## Inside the Parc des Princes

**Away section:** The visiting fans are in a section of the north end. The view is good, directly behind the goal.

**Atmosphere:** PSG home matches have a specific energy — the ultras in the Boulogne and Auteuil ends are organised and vocal, particularly for Champions League. For big knockout matches, the atmosphere is excellent.

**Food and drink inside:** Correct French stadium standard, which means acceptable but not remarkable. Alcohol is not served inside the stadium during the match — this is standard French football practice.

**Security:** French stadium security has become stricter in recent years. ID checks, thorough bag searches, arrive with time. For Champions League matches allow 60 minutes from when you enter the stadium area.

---

## After the match

The metro back into Paris fills up quickly after full time. The main options:

1. **Wait 20-30 minutes** inside the stadium area before joining the metro — shorter queues
2. **Walk** to a further metro station (10-15 minutes) for less crowded trains

Away fans are usually released promptly after the final whistle. The police presence outside is heavy for big matches — walk directly to the metro or follow steward directions.

---

## Where to stay in Paris

**Saint-Germain / 6th arrondissement** — excellent for first-time visitors, central, good restaurants. Expensive.

**Marais / 3rd-4th** — excellent neighbourhood, slightly more affordable than Saint-Germain, great for walking around.

**Near Gare du Nord** — practical if you're arriving by Eurostar from London. Less interesting neighbourhood but cheaper and convenient.

For Champions League semifinals at the Parc des Princes: Paris hotels during these matches fill up fast and prices spike. Book early on a cancellable rate.

---

## The honest summary

A Champions League match at the Parc des Princes, with Paris around it, is one of the better away trip experiences in European football. The city does the heavy lifting — two days in Paris before or after the match makes the trip genuinely worthwhile regardless of the result.

The stadium is good, transport is easy, and the city is exceptional. Sort your ticket early and enjoy Paris.
